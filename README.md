# Sparc Reviver: Play Sparc VR Multiplayer Online Again 🥏🚀

**Sparc Reviver** is a custom client and launcher designed to restore online multiplayer matchmaking for the classic VR sports game, *Sparc*. 

When the official servers shut down, the community was left without a way to play. This project provides free, 24/7 dedicated VPS servers, a global chat system, and a custom launcher so you can get back into the arena and play Sparc VR online with your friends.

## ✨ Features
* **Active Matchmaking:** Browse and join live, 24/7 dedicated servers across US and EU regions.
* **Global Chat & Friends List:** Talk with other players, add friends, and send direct game invites.
* **Custom Avatar Support:** Save and load your custom in-game avatars directly from the launcher.
* **Live Leaderboards:** Track your playtime and see who is dominating the weekly and all-time charts.

## 📥 How to Install and Play

Getting back into the game is simple. 

1. **Download the Latest Release:** Go to the [Releases page](../../releases) and download the latest `Sparc_Reviver.exe` file.
2. **Place in Game Folder:** Move the downloaded `.exe` file directly into your main *Sparc* game installation folder (it must be placed right next to your `Sparc.exe` file).
3. **Launch the Client:** Double-click `Sparc_Reviver.exe`.
4. **Set Your Gamertag:** Enter your desired player name in the top-left corner.
5. **Enter the Arena:** Click the green **Enter Arena** button to quick-join a match, or select a specific server from the Arena Browser on the right!

> **Note on Antivirus/Firewall:** Because this is a custom networking tool, Windows Defender or your antivirus might flag it. This is a false positive. You may need to "Allow" it through your firewall so it can connect to the matchmaking database and voice chat servers.

## 🛠️ Troubleshooting & FAQ

**"The game launches, but I'm stuck on a loading screen!"**
This usually means your Windows Firewall is blocking the game from connecting to the server. Open "Windows Defender Firewall", go to "Inbound Rules", and ensure UDP Ports `7777` and `7778` are allowed. 

**"My voice chat drops out after 5 minutes."**
This is often caused by your router's "UDP Flood Protection" or "Advanced Security" settings mistaking the voice chat packets for an attack. Try temporarily lowering your router's security level or disabling UDP flood protection.
